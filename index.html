Q&A

Q1

List three main differences between web1, web2 and web3 - use the material learned in lectures and on the Internet. Hebrew is recommended but not mandatory.

Answer
  1. Interactivity and User Participation

    2. Web1.0 was static, with limited interactivity. Web2.0 introduced interactive experiences through social media and blogging. Web3.0 aims to enhance user participation and interactivity by leveraging decentralized technologies like blockchain and peer-to-peer networks, allowing greater control over data and interactions.

  2. Data Ownership and Privacy

    3. Web1.0 and Web2.0 emphasized data ownership and privacy, with centralized entities controlling personal data. Web2.0 raised concerns about privacy due to increased sharing on platforms. Web3.0 uses decentralized technologies, like blockchain, to empower users and protect their data, reducing reliance on centralized platforms.

  3. Decentralization and Trustlessness

    4. Web1.0 and Web2.0 were characterized by centralized control, trust in centralized authorities, and user-generated content. Web3.0 aims to decentralize online interactions, using decentralized technologies like blockchain and smart contracts. These technologies enable peer-to-peer transactions, enhancing security and transparency, and reducing reliance on trusted intermediaries.

Answer
  1. Web1.0

    2. In Web1.0, vintage watch buyers rely on static websites like eBay or Sotheby's, where descriptions are limited. Interaction with sellers is limited to email or phone, and transactions involve offline negotiations and payment methods. Researching watch authenticity is limited.

  2. Web2.0

    3. Web2.0 allows users to explore various vintage watch platforms, including online marketplaces like Chrono24, social media platforms like Instagram, and watch enthusiast forums.     4. Web3.0 could enable users to buy vintage watches through decentralized marketplaces and blockchain-based authentication services.
